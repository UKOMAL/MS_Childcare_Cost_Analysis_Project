Geographic Analysis

State-wise Cost Distribution

Childcare costs vary dramatically by region, with the Northeast and West Coast showing significantly higher rates. Missing data for Indiana and New Mexico was derived from neighboring states' averages.

Key Findings

Areas with higher childcare costs show lower workforce participation rates among parents. Our multi-step imputation approach ensured that all states, including those with missing data, could be analyzed.

How We Analyzed the Data

To ensure accuracy and meaningful insights, we:

  • Adjusted all costs to 2018 dollars for fair comparison
  • Combined data from state and federal sources
  • Cross-checked our findings with state reports
  • Tested relationships between different factors
  • Implemented a multi-step imputation approach for missing values:
    • First attempt: Use exact values from the state and year when available
    • Second attempt: Use the mean from the same state across different years
    • Third attempt: For states with completely missing data (notably Indiana and New Mexico), derive values from neighboring states
    • Final attempt: Use the national mean for any remaining missing values
  • Calculated derived metrics including annual costs, cost burden as a percentage of median household income, and working parent ratios
  • Applied consistent visualization approaches using cubehelix color palettes for improved readability and aesthetic consistency

Data Quality Considerations

Our analysis addressed several data challenges:

  • The original dataset contained significant missing data in key childcare columns (31.7% missing for center-based care)
  • States like Indiana and New Mexico required special handling due to missing data
  • For Indiana, we utilized data from neighboring states including Illinois ($2,920), Kentucky ($1,530), Michigan ($1,897), and Ohio ($1,857)
  • For New Mexico, we incorporated data from Arizona ($1,900), Colorado ($2,713), Oklahoma ($1,999), and Texas ($1,310)
  • Our careful imputation process ensured these states were properly represented while maintaining analytical integrity
